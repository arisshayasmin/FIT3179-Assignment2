Beyond the Olympics History

The most awaited international sports festival is around the corner, and fans from every corner of the world are as enthusiastic as ever. As of 2020, 29 Summer Olympics and 22 Winter Olympics had taken place, for a total of 51 Olympic events since 1896.

Lets delve deeper into the history!

Total Number of Medals Won by Each Country in Summer and Winter Olympics

With 2959 medals in total (Summer and Winter Olympics combined), the United States of America remains the leading country worldwide in terms of medal count (1173 of them gold). Moreover, with 12,434 medals, Europe currently is the leading continent with the highest medal count to date. North and South America together have earned around 4000 medals, placing them as the second-place continent up to this point.

Total Number of Medals each Continent's Nations have collectively Won

With an astounding total of 1,1192 medals won to date, Europe leads all other continents in the number of medals won.

Even though the United States consistently leads all other countries in the number of medals won at the Olympics, North America, with a total of 3965 medals, comes in second place to Europe.

As for the other continents, we can see that Asia is third with a total of 2248 medals in total, and Oceania, Africa and South America comes in fourth, fifth and sixth respectively.

What Happened in the recent Tokyo Olympics 2020?

After the COVID-19 pandemic and a year of postponement, every nation and team gave it their all for the first Olympic to take place, and they are just as fired up as ever! With the addition of new sports and events, Tokyo 2020 will have the most events in Olympic history, with a total of 339 events spread across just 33 sports.
